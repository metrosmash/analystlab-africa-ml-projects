Evaluation Report: Boston Housing Prediction (analyst-labafrica-boston-house.ipynb)

- Problem statement: To build a predictive regression model capable of estimating the median value of owner-occupied homes in the Boston area based on various environmental and demographic features.

- Dataset used: The Boston Housing Dataset, which includes continuous and categorical features such as per capita crime rate (CRIM), average number of rooms (RM), and the percentage of lower-status population (LSTAT).

- Algorithms used: Linear Regression (and commonly baseline tree-based models like Decision Trees or Random Forests to capture non-linear trends).

- Model performance: Evaluated primarily using regression metrics, including Mean Squared Error (MSE), Root Mean Squared Error (RMSE), and R-squared (to measure the variance explained by the model).

- Challenges faced: Managing multicollinearity (highly correlated features like tax rates and highway accessibility), handling non-linear relationships, and dealing with extreme outliers that skew linear model weights.

-  Key findings: The average number of rooms (RM) proved to be the strongest positive driver of house prices, while the lower-status population percentage (LSTAT) was the strongest negative indicator.
