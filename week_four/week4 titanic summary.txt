Evaluation Report: Titanic Survival Prediction (analystlab-titanic-noteboook-week4.ipynb)

- Problem statement: To build a binary classification model that accurately predicts whether a passenger survived the Titanic shipwreck based on their socio-demographic data and travel information.

- Dataset used: The classic Titanic Dataset, containing passenger details such as class (Pclass), sex, age, ticket fare, and sibling/spouse counts (SibSp).

- Algorithms used: Logistic Regression (often compared against models like Random Forest or Gradient Boosting).

- Model performance: Evaluated using standard classification metrics, specifically Accuracy, Precision, Recall, and the F1-Score to account for class imbalances.

- Challenges faced: Handling massive chunks of missing data (especially in the Age and Cabin columns) and engineering categorical text variables (like titles or ticket prefixes) into clean, machine-readable numeric formats.

- Key findings: Survival was highly dependent on specific demographics and socioeconomic status. Females, younger passengers, and those with First-Class tickets had a dramatically higher probability of survival compared to adult males in Third Class.
